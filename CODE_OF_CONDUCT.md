# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Relegate is a welcoming project for everyone, regardless of experience level,
background, identity, or coding methods. Whether you are filing your first issue
or your hundredth PR, you belong here.

We are committed to providing a friendly, safe, and harassment-free experience
for all participants.

## Expected Behavior

- **Be kind and constructive** in issues, pull requests, and discussions.
- **Assume good intent.** If something reads ambiguously, give the benefit of
  the doubt.
- **Give helpful, specific feedback.** "This doesn't work" is less useful than
  "This fails when X because Y."
- **Respect differing viewpoints.** Disagreement is fine; dismissiveness is not.
- **Welcome newcomers.** Answer questions patiently, point people to docs, and
  remember that everyone starts somewhere.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- **Personal attacks, insults, or dismissive language** directed at any
  participant.
- **Harassment of any kind**, including unwelcome comments related to personal
  characteristics or identity.
- **Posting others' private information** (email addresses, credentials, real
  names) without explicit consent.
- **Sustained disruption** of discussions, issues, or pull requests (spam,
  off-topic derailing, bad-faith arguments).
- **Trolling or deliberately inflammatory comments** intended to provoke rather
  than contribute.

## Enforcement

The project maintainer reserves the right to:

- Edit or remove comments, commits, issues, or other contributions that violate
  this Code of Conduct.
- Temporarily or permanently block any contributor who engages in unacceptable
  behavior.

Enforcement decisions are made at the maintainer's discretion and are final.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Any other communication channels associated with the project
- Public spaces where an individual is representing the project

## Contact

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by opening
an issue on the [Relegate repository](https://github.com/gbroeckling/relegate/issues).
If the issue involves a specific participant, you may also reach out to the
maintainer directly through GitHub.

All reports will be reviewed and addressed.
